What Do Riders Actually Say About Waymo?

An analysis of 3,075 Google Play Store reviews reveals that riders love the smoothness but are frustrated by wait times. Trust is growing, but two specific pain points keep showing up.

By Michelle Chan
April 3, 2026

After analyzing 3,075 rider reviews from the Google Play Store spanning April 2025 through March 2026, a clear picture emerges of how the public actually feels about Waymo's autonomous ride-hailing service. The data tells a story of growing trust, genuine enthusiasm for the technology, and two persistent friction points that the product team should prioritize.

Across six cities and seven topic categories, the overall sentiment is positive: 0.235 on a compound scale from -1 to +1, with 59.8% of reviews classified as positive. But averages can be deceiving. The story is in the details.

The Big Picture

Sentiment has been climbing, with one notable exception

Monthly sentiment trended upward over the 12-month period, driven by expanding service areas and growing rider familiarity. The most significant disruption came in December 2025, when a holiday surge pricing controversy triggered a sharp but short-lived dip in rider satisfaction.

What Riders Talk About

They love the smoothness. They hate the wait.

Wait times and availability dominate the conversation, accounting for 25% of all reviews, and it is the only topic category with net-negative sentiment. Ride quality, by contrast, scores the highest sentiment of any topic. Safety and trust sentiment has been growing steadily as riders accumulate more trips.

What Do Riders Actually Say About Waymo?

An analysis of 4,134 real app store reviews. Riders love the experience. The data shows clearly where things need work.

By Michelle Chan  |  Data Scientist, Market Expansion Analytics
April 2026  ·  Sources: Google Play Store & Apple App Store verified reviews

Looking at 4,134 real reviews from Google Play and the Apple App Store, a pretty clear picture emerges. Riders who actually get in a Waymo tend to love it. The experience of riding in an autonomous vehicle is novel, smooth, and by most accounts genuinely impressive. Where things fall apart is everything around the ride itself. Getting picked up, using the app, finding service in your neighborhood. That is where the frustration lives.

There is also something genuinely surprising in the data. Riders in cities where Waymo does not yet operate are leaving reviews to ask for it by name. Seattle, Denver, New York, and Chicago show up organically in review text, almost always from people who rode Waymo while visiting somewhere else and came home wanting it. That kind of unprompted demand is hard to manufacture.

Section 1
Android vs iOS: Two Very Different Audiences

iOS users rate Waymo noticeably higher than Android users. The 365 iOS reviews skew heavily toward 5 stars. Android users, a much larger group at 3,769 reviews, show a more mixed picture with more mid-range ratings and detailed complaints about wait times and app reliability.

Insight: iOS users tend to fit Waymo's core demographic pretty closely: higher-income, urban, and already comfortable with new technology. The Android base is broader and more diverse, which means higher expectations in some areas and different frustrations altogether.

Section 2
What Riders Actually Talk About

Reviews cluster naturally into 11 topic categories. Ride quality dominates the conversation at 32% of all reviews, with an average rating of 4.80★. Future tech sentiment is nearly perfect at 4.98★. At the other end, app reliability (3.40★) and coverage area (3.39★) are where riders express the most frustration.

Section 4
Current Market Sentiment by City

Los Angeles leads in both review volume and satisfaction among the established markets. Phoenix and San Francisco are the oldest markets and score a bit lower, which makes sense. Riders who have been using Waymo for a while tend to have higher expectations and are quicker to flag things that are not quite right. Houston, Dallas, and Orlando are the newest additions and their 5.0 star averages reflect that first-time excitement from early adopters.

Section 5
Where Riders Want Waymo Next

A real subset of reviews mention cities where Waymo does not yet operate. These are not survey responses or prompted feedback. They are riders who experienced Waymo somewhere else and came home wanting it. That is a different kind of signal.

Insight: Seattle comes up four times with a 4.25 star average. Denver and Chicago each appear three times, both averaging a perfect 5 stars. Every single one of these mentions comes from someone who rode Waymo somewhere it already operates and is asking for it in their own city.

Section 6
International Demand & the Tourist Signal

International locations appear organically in reviews, almost entirely from tourists who rode Waymo while visiting US cities. London/UK is the most mentioned international market but the low average score is misleading. It has nothing to do with the quality of the rides. The problem is a technical one: the app rejects non-US phone numbers and credit cards during signup, which means international visitors get blocked before they can even request a car.

6 reviews explicitly describe being blocked by UK phone number validation. Every rider who got through left a 5★ review.

Insight: The UK phone and payment issue is turning away international tourists before they ever get in a car. Every rider who did manage to get through left a 5 star review. Fixing international number support is a straightforward product change with an outsized impact on perception.
